I'm Khushbu Patel, a bioinformatics scientist based in Georgia, currently working at The Children's Hospital of Philadelphia.

What I do? (For technical folks)

The overarching goal is to build on my extensive experience with multi-omics data from pediatric cancers, applying statistical and computational methods, understanding genomic landscape to identify critical gene and protein targets and understand the underlying mechanisms of pediatric cancers in order to develop effective therapies and minimize long-term side effects.

Okay...for you non-technical amigos

I use cool computer algorithms and write code to make sense out of long strings of letters or large matrices containing thousands of rows and multiple columns with numbers (multi-omics data) to generate valuable insights and report scholarly findings, which are often a part of peer-reviewed manuscripts. These findings add to the knowledge base and can be a useful resource to build targeted therapies. Make sense?
